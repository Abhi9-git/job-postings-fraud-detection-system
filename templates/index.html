Critical Assessment: Why are models scoring 100%?

Both Logistic Regression and Random Forest models show nearly 100% accuracy, precision, and recall on the test partition. In real-world data science, perfect scores almost always point to two common factors:

  • Target Leakage: Specific features contained within the structured data strongly reveal the class label (e.g. corporate domain naming conventions vs gmail accounts).
  • Synthetic Patterns: The job description and details in the dataset might follow a predictable structured formula since they are generated, making them easily segmentable by standard vectorizers.

Key Feature Analysis & Insights

During exploratory data analysis, several features stood out as incredibly strong discriminative signals:

  • Gmail Domains (is_gmail): In the dataset, authentic job postings are almost exclusively sent from corporate domains (e.g., hr@company.com). Conversely, nearly 85% of fraudulent job postings use free webmail providers (primarily Gmail) to collect resumes.
  • Company Logo Presence (has_logo): Scammers and fake organizations rarely upload company profile logos. In this dataset, there is a massive discrepancy: valid jobs feature company logos at a significantly higher rate than fake ones.
  • Text Density & Complexity: Fake job listings tend to have shorter company profiles, vague descriptions, and copy-pasted benefit templates, leading to easily identifiable cluster patterns in the TF-IDF feature space.

How the Models Were Trained

The prediction server matches the original pipeline designed during the notebooks research phase:

  1. NLP Vectorizer: TF-IDF fits the combined text of description, requirements, benefits, and company profiles, extracting the top 500 features (unigrams and bigrams).
  2. Feature Stacking: The 500 TF-IDF features are horizontally stacked (using scipy.sparse.hstack) with 12 structured numerical and encoded categorical metadata attributes.
  3. Train-Test Partitioning: A stratified split ensures the balanced 50/50 proportion of real and fake classes is preserved across training (2,400 samples) and testing (600 samples).
